| #  | Truth | Status | Evidence |

| 1  | YouTube audio stops when user switches to Instagram or Home | VERIFIED | `ContentView.swift:49-58` — `.onChange(of: nav.activeScreen)` calls `youtubeState.pauseAllVideos()` when old value is `.youtube` |
| 2  | Instagram video stops when user switches to YouTube or Home | VERIFIED | Same `.onChange` block calls `instagramState.pauseAllVideos()` when old value is `.instagram` |

| 4  | FloatingPillButton is not visible on the settings screen | VERIFIED | Settings is rendered inside `HomeScreenView`'s `NavigationStack` — `activeScreen` remains `.home`, so `activePlatform` is `nil` and pill is absent |
| 5  | FloatingPillButton is visible when viewing Instagram or YouTube | VERIFIED | Same conditional — `activePlatform` is non-nil on `.instagram` and `.youtube` |
| 6  | Platform web content extends to the bottom edge with no gap | VERIFIED | `ContentView.swift:18,23` — `.ignoresSafeArea(.container, edges: .bottom)` applied to both `PlatformTabView` instances |
| 7  | Dragging the pill moves it 1:1 with the finger during the drag | VERIFIED | `FloatingPillButton.swift:118-119` — `.onChanged` sets `dragOffset = value.translation` with no animation wrapper |
| 8  | Releasing the pill after a drag animates it smoothly to its clamped position with a spring | VERIFIED | `FloatingPillButton.swift:131-142` — unclamped position set and `dragOffset` cleared in same frame, then `withAnimation(.spring(response: 0.35, dampingFraction: 0.8))` animates to clamped position. `reduceMotion` path sets directly without animation. |
| 9  | Expanding the pill near the right edge causes it to expand leftward (toward center) | VERIFIED | `FloatingPillButton.swift:32-34` — `expandsLeft` is `true` when `nav.pillPosition.x > UIScreen.main.bounds.width / 2`; offset is `-(expandedPillWidth / 2 - pillSize / 2)` = -58pt |
| 10 | Expanding the pill near the left edge causes it to expand rightward (toward center) | VERIFIED | Same offset block — when `expandsLeft` is `false`, offset is `+(expandedPillWidth / 2 - pillSize / 2)` = +58pt |
| 11 | Expanded pill never clips off-screen | VERIFIED | Direction-aware 58pt offset keeps the 172pt pill anchored at the collapsed pill center and grows away from the nearest edge. The collapsed pill's own clamped bounds via `clampedPosition` ensure the anchor point itself is always on-screen. |

### Human Verification Required

#### 1. YouTube Audio Cutoff on Switch

**Test:** Open YouTube in the app, start a video with audio, then navigate to Instagram or Home via the pill.
**Expected:** YouTube audio stops immediately. Returning to YouTube shows the video paused at the same position.
**Why human:** The `evaluateJavaScript` call and `didSet` wiring are correct in code but actual audio cutoff requires a live WKWebView and a playing video.

#### 2. Pill Absent on Home and Settings Screens

**Test:** Launch the app (home screen), confirm no pill is visible. Tap Settings from the home screen, confirm no pill is visible there either.
**Expected:** FloatingPillButton does not appear on either the home screen or settings screen.
**Why human:** Conditional rendering is correct; visual confirmation requires running the app.

#### 3. Spring Drag Release — No Teleport Snap

**Test:** On a platform screen, drag the pill to a new location and release.
**Expected:** The pill springs smoothly from the drop point to the clamped safe position. No instant position jump.
**Why human:** Animation behavior requires touch input on a running simulator or device.

#### 4. Expanded Pill Direction Near Edges

**Test:** Drag the pill to the right edge of the screen, tap it to expand.
**Expected:** The expanded HStack grows leftward (toward center). Repeat with the pill on the left side — it should grow rightward.
**Why human:** Pixel-level clipping can only be confirmed visually.

#### 5. Platform Content Edge-to-Edge at Bottom

**Test:** Open Instagram or YouTube, scroll to a page with content near the bottom.
**Expected:** Web content fills to the bottom of the screen with no visible gap between content and the screen edge.
**Why human:** Safe area layout behavior is visual and requires a device/simulator.
